## 2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet, or other electronic device) when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used by website owners to make their websites function more efficiently and to provide reporting information.

### 2.1 Types of Cookies by Origin

**First-Party Cookies:**  
These are cookies set by Mello AI Ltd directly. We use them to provide core functionality and improve your experience on our Services.

**Third-Party Cookies:**  
These are cookies set by parties other than Mello AI Ltd, such as analytics providers, advertising networks, or social media platforms. These cookies enable third-party features and functionality on our Services.

### 2.2 Types of Cookies by Duration

**Session Cookies:**  
These cookies are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser. They help us remember your actions during a single browsing session.

**Persistent Cookies:**  
These cookies remain on your device for a set period or until you manually delete them. They help us recognize you when you return to our Services and remember your preferences.

## 3. Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use other tracking technologies:

### 3.1 Web Beacons (Pixels)
Small transparent images embedded in web pages or emails that track whether content has been viewed or interacted with.

### 3.2 Local Storage
Technology that stores data locally on your browser or device, enabling us to store information for performance optimization.

### 3.3 Software Development Kits (SDKs)
Code that enables third-party features in mobile applications, which may collect usage and performance data.

### 3.4 JavaScript and Similar Technologies
Scripts that run on your device to enable interactive features and collect usage information.

## 6. Your Cookie Choices and Controls

You have several options to manage and control cookies:

### 6.1 Cookie Consent Manager

When you first visit our website, you will see a cookie consent banner allowing you to:
- Accept all cookies
- Reject non-essential cookies
- Customize your cookie preferences by category

You can change your cookie preferences at any time by:
- Clicking the "Cookie Settings" link in our website footer
- Accessing the cookie preference center in your account settings
- Emailing us at: admin@heymello.ai

### 6.2 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:
- Block all cookies
- Accept only first-party cookies
- Delete existing cookies
- Set preferences for specific websites

**How to Manage Cookies in Common Browsers:**

**Google Chrome:**
1. Go to Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
2. Choose your preferred cookie settings

**Mozilla Firefox:**
1. Go to Settings > Privacy & Security
2. Under Cookies and Site Data, choose your preferences

**Safari:**
1. Go to Preferences > Privacy
2. Choose your cookie and tracking preferences

**Microsoft Edge:**
1. Go to Settings > Cookies and site permissions
2. Manage cookie settings

**Note:** Blocking or deleting cookies may affect your ability to use certain features of our Services.

### 6.3 Third-Party Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of interest-based advertising using these resources:
- **Your Online Choices (EU):** https://www.youronlinechoices.com
- **Network Advertising Initiative:** https://www.networkadvertising.org/choices/
- **Digital Advertising Alliance:** https://www.aboutads.info/choices/
- **Google Ads Settings:** https://adssettings.google.com

### 6.4 Mobile Device Settings

For mobile devices, you can control tracking through:
- **iOS:** Settings > Privacy > Advertising > Limit Ad Tracking
- **Android:** Settings > Google > Ads > Opt out of Ads Personalization

### 6.5 Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals websites not to track your online activity. Currently, there is no universal standard for how to respond to DNT signals. However, we respect your privacy choices and provide robust cookie control options as described above.

## 7. Consequences of Disabling Cookies

If you choose to disable or block cookies, certain features of our Services may not function properly:

**Essential Cookies:**
- You will not be able to log in or access secure areas
- Security features may be compromised
- Some core functionality may not work

**Performance Cookies:**
- We cannot improve user experience based on usage patterns
- Technical issues may take longer to identify and resolve

**Functionality Cookies:**
- Your preferences will not be saved between visits
- Personalization features will not be available
- You may need to re-enter information on each visit

**Targeting/Advertising Cookies:**
- You will still see advertisements, but they will be less relevant to your interests
- You may see the same ads repeatedly
